Why Ree exists

You have been doing.
And doing. And doing.

The work, the home, the people who depend on you — you hold it all up, gracefully, and somewhere along the way the tiredness stopped going away with sleep.

That is not weakness. That is what happens when everything flows out and nothing flows back in.

A holiday distracts you from it, for a while. Ree does something else entirely: it teaches you to replenish from within, through practices that Indian traditional sciences have refined over centuries — so that the steadiness returns home with you, and stays.

The approach

Four sciences. One immersion.

Not a spa getaway. Not a generic yoga weekend. A cultural immersion into traditions refined over centuries — taught by lifelong practitioners of the Sadhguru Gurukulam lineage.

i

Classical Hatha Yoga

Alignment-based practice that works on the physical, psychological, and spiritual dimensions simultaneously. Taught as a complete system, not a fitness routine.

ii

Kalaripayattu

The mother of all martial arts, originating from the Dhanurveda. Three sets of practices to enhance flexibility, balance, and inner strength.

iii

Chanting & Music

From sound to silence — Hindustani music and traditional chants that settle the nervous system in ways words cannot reach.

iv

Classical Arts

Craft, movement, and a traditional Devi celebration — beauty and culture as an essential part of the medicine.

Research & Evidence

Not belief.
Data.

Yoga-based interventions have been studied extensively in peer-reviewed literature. The evidence is clear, measurable, and growing.

14
Randomized Controlled Trials
reviewed in latest meta-analysis
International Journal of Yoga · Systematic Review · 2025

Yoga Interventions on Mental Health of Nursing Professionals

A PRISMA-compliant systematic review of 14 RCTs (967 participants, predominantly female, ages 18–69) evaluating yoga's impact on stress, anxiety, burnout, depression, and sleep quality.

9 of 11 studies showed significant stress reduction; all 3 burnout studies reported significant decrease in emotional exhaustion and depersonalization.

Cocchiara et al., 2019; Hilcove et al., 2021; Zok et al., 2024

Journal of Holistic Nursing · RCT · 2021

Mindfulness-Based Yoga for Stress and Burnout in Healthcare Workers

80 healthcare professionals randomly assigned to mindfulness-based yoga vs. control. Measured via Perceived Stress Scale, Maslach Burnout Inventory, salivary cortisol, and blood pressure.

Statistically significant improvements (p < .01) in stress, burnout, vitality, sleep quality, serenity, and mindfulness in the yoga group vs. control.

Hilcove, Marceau, Thekdi et al., J Holist Nurs 2021

Frontiers in Public Health · Pilot Study · 2024

Reducing Stress and Burnout Risk Through Yoga Techniques

Investigated physiological mechanisms: yoga modulates the HPA axis, lowers cortisol, enhances parasympathetic activity, and stimulates endorphin, dopamine, and serotonin release.

Yoga's mind-body approach outperforms other exercise and non-exercise interventions for stress management and burnout prevention.

Zok, Matecka, Bienkowski et al., Front Public Health 2024
