Why Ree exists

You have been doing.
And doing. And doing.

The work, the home, the people who depend on you — you hold it all up, gracefully, and somewhere along the way the tiredness stopped going away with sleep. That is not weakness. That is what happens when everything flows out and nothing flows back in.

A holiday distracts you from it, for a while. Ree does something else entirely: it teaches you to replenish from within, through practices that Indian traditional sciences have refined over centuries — so that the steadiness returns home with you, and stays.

The approach

Four sciences. One immersion.

Not a spa getaway. Not a generic yoga weekend. A cultural immersion into traditions refined over centuries — taught by lifelong practitioners of the Isha Samskriti lineage.

i.

Classical Hatha Yoga

Practice taught from the very basics, the classical way — to steady the body and quiet a mind that has forgotten how to rest.

ii.

Kalari

Kerala's ancient martial art — strength, alertness, and play, taught by a national medalist. Introductory, and safe for complete beginners.

iii.

Chanting & Music

From sound to silence — Hindustani music and traditional chants that settle the nervous system in ways words cannot.

iv.

Classical Arts

Craft, movement, and a traditional Devi celebration — beauty and culture as an essential part of the medicine.

Yoga-based interventions show measurable effects on burnout and stress markers in peer-reviewed research.123

Questions

Asked, answered

Who is this retreat for? ＋
Women, 18 and above, who feel stretched thin by the pace of their lives — professionals, homemakers, teachers, anyone. No prior experience with yoga, Kalari, or music is needed.
I've never done yoga or Kalari. Can I still join? ＋
Yes. Every session is taught from the basics and adapted to the group. Kalari sessions are introductory and safe for beginners.
How fit do I need to be? ＋
If you can manage a brisk walk and sit on the floor, you're fine. If you have a medical condition, injury, or are pregnant, tell us during your enquiry call so we can advise.
What is the food like? ＋
Vegetarian, fresh, and light — designed to support the practices. Think khichdi, seasonal fruit, sprouts, millet dishes, soups. Tell us about allergies when you book.
What are the rooms like? ＋
Twin-sharing, air-conditioned rooms with hot water at Tavaro Resorts, Kokapet. If you'd prefer a single room, ask during your enquiry call — subject to availability and an additional charge.
What should I bring? ＋
Comfortable clothing you can move in, walking shoes, a water bottle, personal medication, and one traditional outfit for the Day 3 Devi session. Yoga mats are provided.
What happens after the three days? ＋
The retreat includes a 40-day post-program engagement — structured follow-through so the practices settle into your daily life rather than fading by the following week.
How do I book? ＋
Fill in the enquiry form. We'll call you within 24 hours, answer your questions, and share a payment link to confirm your seat. Seats are confirmed on payment, in order of payment received. The cohort is capped at 15.
Can I get a refund if I can't make it? ＋
Full refund minus a 5% processing fee up to 14 days before; 50% up to 7 days before; no refund within 7 days, though you may transfer your seat to another woman or to a future cohort. The full policy is in the footer below.
